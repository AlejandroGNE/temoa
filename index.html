Welcome to The TEMOA Project

Energy affects everyone. Should not everyone have access to the same tools and assumptions the policy analysts and makers do? The Temoa (“teh-moe-uh”) Project aims to do exactly that, by providing an open source energy model and engendering a community of interoperability and transparency.

Energy economy optimization (EEO) models are “abstract worlds” where mathematics enforce various laws, such as the basic requirement that a process may not create more energy than it is fed. The worlds they create are “abstract” because a researcher only has to define a set of characteristics (e.g. the price of a barrel of oil in 2015, the maximum percentage of power that may come from nuclear options, the total demand for residential heating) and a goal to attain (e.g. minimum system cost). The researcher does not have to create rules afresh for each hypothetical exploration, nor does he need to keep track of all intermediate steps: in essence, the only data a researcher must provide are the system inputs (e.g. energy sources like coal, oil, natural gas) and the outputs the system must meet (e.g. expected residental heating in 2015). The model fills in the necessary intermediate steps based on the mathematical rules.
